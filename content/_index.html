This is a work-in-progress prototype for a database website for the Oxford Centre for Hindu Studies.

Features

Phase 1

A browsable library, where users can

  • see transliterated and translated texts side-by-side with images of the original manuscripts
  • overlay text on top of the manuscript image, to easily compare e.g. the transliteration with the original sanskrit
  • view images in full screen, and toggle between medium and high resolution
  • download individual texts and images on the manuscript pages
  • comment on text and images, and e.g. or suggest corrections. This is done with comment-only links to google sheets with a manuscript’s content

Demo content

On the current prototype site, a few initial manuscripts demonstrate these features:

  • Netratantra demonstrates the layout when we have both photos and text
  • Haṭha[yoga]pradīpikā demonstrates the layout when we have only text
  • Durgāsaptaśatī, Svasthānī, and the Palm leaf manuscripts demonstrates the layout when we have only photos

Here will be a video with a short demo

Phase 2

An analysis app, where users can

  • perform basic text analysis (e.g., count and compare the frequency of specific words or phrases across manuscripts)
  • render formatted HTML, PDF, or Word files of specific manuscripts
  • bulk download texts or images for manuscripts that meet specific criteria
  • download specific content that meet specific criteria (e.g., get just the transliterations of text from a certain period)

These features will make this project distinct to the existing alternatives, such as GRETIL or Muktabodha
